Chicken Cacciatore

Description:

Chicken Cacciatore is a rustic Italian dish that combines tender chicken simmered in a savory tomato-based sauce with onions, peppers, mushrooms, and herbs. The name “cacciatore” means “hunter” in Italian, and this dish is traditionally prepared hunter-style: hearty, simple, and full of flavor. Perfect for serving with pasta, rice, or crusty bread to soak up the rich sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (or breasts)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 8 oz (225 g) mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz / 800 g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth (or dry red wine)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Steps:

  1. Season the chicken with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil and brown the chicken on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté onions, peppers, mushrooms, and garlic until softened.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes, chicken broth (or wine), oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes. Stir well.
  5. Return the chicken to the skillet, nestling it into the sauce.
  6. Cover and simmer on low heat for 30–40 minutes, until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot with pasta, rice, or bread.
